EMI Prediction App

AI-Powered FinTech Solution for Loan Risk Assessment

Project: FinTech ML Suite

Problem Statement

Challenges in Traditional Loan Assessment:

Financial institutions face significant challenges with manual loan approval processes:

  • Time-consuming and labor-intensive evaluation procedures
  • Prone to human error and subjective bias in decision making
  • Inconsistent risk assessment criteria across different evaluators
  • Limited predictive capabilities for future loan performance
  • Customers face uncertainty about their maximum affordable EMI amounts
  • Lack of data-driven insights for personalized lending decisions

Business Impact:

Manual processes result in delayed approvals, increased operational costs, and higher default rates due to inadequate risk assessment.

Project Overview

The EMI Prediction App is a comprehensive AI-powered platform designed to revolutionize loan risk assessment in the financial sector. This end-to-end machine learning solution combines advanced predictive analytics with an intuitive web interface to provide accurate loan eligibility predictions and maximum EMI calculations.

Key Features:

  • Dual ML approach with classification and regression models
  • Streamlit-based web application with multi-page interface
  • MLflow integration for experiment tracking and model versioning
  • Production-ready deployment with monitoring capabilities
  • Comprehensive borrower profiling with 27+ features

Data Analysis & EDA

Top Drivers of Eligibility (Positive Factors)

  • max_monthly_emi (Strong Correlation: 0.59): The most powerful indicator. A higher calculated EMI capacity almost guarantees approval, underscoring the focus on affordability.
  • Professional and Post Graduate Education: These groups have significantly higher approval rates (27.0% and 22.4%, respectively) compared to High School graduates (11.1%), showing education is a proxy for financial stability.
  • Own/Family House Type: Applicants who own a house or live with family have ~24.3% approval, while renters have only 9.5% approval, highlighting housing stability as a crucial positive factor.

Key Risk Factors (Negative Impact):

  • requested_amount (Moderate Correlation: -0.31): High loan requests are a major risk. As the requested amount grows, the likelihood of eligibility decreases substantially.
  • High Monthly Expenses: Larger college_fees, school_fees, and monthly_rent reduce disposable income and are associated with lower eligibility.
  • Strict Lending Environment: Overall, 77.3% of all applicants across all loan types are Not_Eligible, indicating a conservative approval process, particularly for Personal and Vehicle loans.

Demographic & Financial Profiles: Where the Insights Lie

Factors with Negligible Impact:

  • Gender: Approval rates are nearly identical (F: ~18.40%, M: ~18.39%).
  • Age: Among active age groups (25-64), approval rates are consistent (~18.2% - 18.5%).
  • Company Type: The specific type of employer (Large Indian, MNC, etc.) has almost no influence on eligibility.

Weak Positive Indicators (Collectively Important):

  • Income & Savings: monthly_salary (0.23), bank_balance (0.26), and emergency_fund (0.24) are all positively correlated with eligibility.
  • Spending & Credit: Indicators like groceries_utilities (0.29) and credit_score (0.17) also show a positive, albeit weak, association.

Model Development

Algorithm Selection & Training Strategy:

Classification Models Evaluated:

  • Logistic Regression (multiple versions with different preprocessing)
  • Random Forest Classifier with ensemble learning
  • XGBoost Classifier with gradient boosting

Regression Models Evaluated:

  • Linear Regression as baseline model
  • Random Forest Regressor for non-linear relationships
  • XGBoost Regressor for complex pattern recognition

Training Process:

  • Cross-validation for robust performance estimation
  • Hyperparameter optimization using grid/random search
  • MLflow experiment tracking for reproducibility
  • Model validation on holdout test sets
